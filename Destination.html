Destinations

Leh

Leh, the heart of Ladakh, is a breathtaking tourist destination nestled in the Himalayas. Known for its crystal-clear blue skies, rugged mountains, and peaceful monasteries, Leh offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure, and spirituality.

Dharamsala

Serenely spiritual Dharamsala is home to the largest Tibetan temple outside of Tibet. It’s known for its religious iconography and is the monastery of the Dalai Lama, who holds public lectures a few times a year. Once you’ve restored your spiritual energy, enjoy a picturesque stroll to Bhagsu Waterfall or climb the hill to bask in stunning views of the Himalayas

Ujjain

One of the seven sacred Hindu cities, Ujjain is located on the banks of river Kshipra and is land of the magnificent Kumbh Mela. Iconic religious sites and a web of lively lanes are a vibrant display of Ujjain’s art and heritage.

Mahabaleshwar

Mahabaleshwar is the ideal hill station getaway from Mumbai with its mesmerising waterfalls, old forts, historic temples and long stretches of vibrant strawberry plantations.

Rameshvaram

Rameswaram is a sacred island town at India's southern tip, famous for the Ramanathaswamy Temple, a major Hindu pilgrimage site linked to the epic Ramayana and one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines, known for its spiritual significance, long corridors, and holy waters. Connected to the mainland by the engineering marvel Pamban Bridge, it's also renowned for its beaches like Dhanushkodi and its rich history, attracting pilgrims and tourists for its divine atmosphere and natural beauty.

Jaipur

Jaipur, India's "Pink City," is Rajasthan's capital, known for its vibrant culture, historic forts (Amber, Nahargarh, Jaigarh), opulent palaces (City Palace, Hawa Mahal, Jal Mahal), astronomical observatory (Jantar Mantar), and bustling bazaars with gems, textiles, and crafts, founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727 as India's first planned city, and is a UNESCO World Heritage site

Kashmir

Kashmir, previously spelled Cashmere, is a region located in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent. It is known for its natural beauty and is a popular destination for tourists. Historically, the name Kashmir referred to the valley just to the south of the western end of the Himalayan mountain range. Today, Kashmir refers to a much larger area that includes the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir (which includes the Kashmir Valley, Jammu region and Ladakh), Azad Kashmir and Gilgit–Baltistan (part of Pakistan), and Aksai Chin and the Trans-Karakoram Tract (part of China). The main "Valley of Kashmir" is a low-fertile area surrounded by mountains and contains many rivers.The region is part of a border dispute between India and Pakistan.

Taj

The Taj Mahal is a stunning ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, between 1631-1653; it's a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture, a symbol of eternal love, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and known for its perfect symmetry, intricate pietra dura inlay work with precious stones, large dome, four minarets, and beautiful Persian-style gardens with reflecting pools.
